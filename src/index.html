Apples

The apple is the pomaceous fruit of the apple tree.

Cripps Pink

Developed in Western Australia, Cripps Pink is a cross of Golden Delicious and Lady Williams.

Empire

Empires premiered in 1966 in the Empire State of New York.

Fuji

Originally developed in Japan in the late 1930s and named after the famous Mt. Fuji, U.S.-grown Fujis began appearing in markets in the 1980s.

Gala

This variety, a cross between Kidd’s Orange Red and Golden Delicious, originated in New Zealand.

Golden Delicious

Yellow with an occasional pink blush, Golden Delicious is “Apple Lite” – loved by those who prefer a mild, sweet flavor.

Granny Smith

This Australian native was discovered in 1868 as a chance seedling by “Granny” Anne Smith of Ryde, New South Wales.

Honeycrisp

This honey of an apple has a honeyed, mild flavor and a crispness deemed explosive.

McIntosh

This old, well-known variety was discovered as a chance seedling by John McIntosh in 1811.

Red Delicious

The most widely recognized of all U.S. apple varieties originated in Iowa in the 1870s.
